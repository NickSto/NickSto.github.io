July 2021 Galactic News

Hello all,

The 2021 Galaxy Community Conference has started! It is too late to register for the 3 day conference next week. It is also officially too late to sign up for the CoFest on 9-10 July, but if you follow this link, you just mind find yourself invited anyway.

PeerJ

PeerJ is an Open Access publisher of seven peer-reviewed journals. Our mission is to give researchers the publishing tools and services they want with a unique and exciting experience. PeerJ offers a unique Lifetime Membership model for Open Access, allowing authors to pay once and publish for life. PeerJ is sponsoring the Best Poster/Demo presentation award at GCC2021.

GalaxyWorks

Need more Galaxy? Galaxy Pro is a managed Galaxy instance with no quotas or queue wait times. Ready to use without management overhead.

Limagrain

An agricultural cooperative held by French farmers, and an international seed group, Limagrain is driven by its purpose: cooperating for the progress of agriculture everywhere, for everyone. Focused on the genetic progress of plants, Limagrain breeds, produces and distributes field seeds, vegetable seeds and agri-food products.

There are new positions in the Galaxy Career Center, in the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, and in the United States. There are also several previously posted positions that are still open, including several with the Galaxy Project in the US.

Open Life Science program & the Galaxy community

Involvement in OLS-3 and invitation to apply to the next cohort

Open Life Science (OLS) is an online mentoring & training program to help individuals and stakeholders in research to become Open Science ambassadors. Galaxy was involved in the first 3 cohorts, and the call for the 4th cohort is now open.

SARS-CoV-2 Data Analysis and Monitoring with Galaxy

9-12 August

The goal of this workshop is to build capacity in SARS-CoV-2 data analysis and data management, including data submission to ENA. After the workshop, all participants will be able to upload viral sequencing data, call all variants, create a variety of reports and create consensus alignments.

It will be a 4-day event introducing scalable and reproducible SARS-CoV-2 data analysis with Galaxy. The sessions will be pre-recorded and provided in advance. During the workshop, there will be live support in chat and live Q&A sessions, in which experts will answer questions.

Releases

Galaxy 21.05

We are pleased to announce the 21.05 Galaxy release.

A new development stack (featuring FastAPI, Celery and Circus) is coming, a job handler update is here, ¡Galaxy, ahora en español!, Remote Files gets FTP Support, and SimText visualization is added.

And there is a lot more in the full user and [developer and admin](https: //docs.galaxyproject.org/en/master/releases/21.05_announce.html) release announcements.

BioBlend 0.16.0

BioBlend is a Python library for interacting with Galaxy and CloudMan APIs. BioBlend 0.16.0 supports the latest 21.05 release of Galaxy, and has the largest number of added features for some time.
